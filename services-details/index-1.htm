The Law Students’ Society (LSS), University of Ibadan (LSS, UI)

  • Background
  • Student Organizations
  • Aims and Objectives

The Law Students’ Society (LSS), University of Ibadan was established in 1983 with her first President being Justice Taiwo O. Taiwo, a Federal High Court Judge. It is the representative body for all law students in the Faculty of Law, University of Ibadan. The society has over seven hundred members from 100 level to 500 level who range from different cultural and ethnic backgrounds, religions and locations all over Nigeria.

Asides the major arms of the faculty, the LSS also includes a plethora of student organizations for the holistic development of the law student. These organizations could be categorized into chambers, religious organizations and also secular organizations. They are:
1. Law Literary and Debating Society
2. Law Chorale
3. The Moot and Mock Society
4. The International Law Students Association
5. Alternative Dispute Resolution Society
6. Lawliwood Society
7. Hezekiah Oladipo Chambers
8. Taslim Olawale Elias Chambers
9. Augustine Nnamani Chambers
10. Darnley Alexander Chambers
11. National Association of Muslim Law Students
12. Christian Law Students’ Fellowship of Nigeria

The aims and the objectives of LSS, U.I. are
1) To cater for the welfare of Law Students within the University of Ibadan.
2) To advance and promote legal studies through seminars, symposium, public lectures, moot and mock trials, quiz and debates, publications and visit to courts.
3) To inculcate discipline and a sense of belonging in Law Students within the University of Ibadan.
4) To ensure cooperation and exchange of legal ideas between faculties of law in Nigeria and other countries.

Similar to any democratic organization, The Law students society also comprises a defined structure.
There exists the primary organs of the Law Students' Society with their powers, functions and duties provided by the LSS Constitution (2021) as amended.

Essentially, these organs are the LSRC, the Executive arm and The Judiciary.
Other notable organizations include the Law Press organization and the 13 registered organizations within the faculty.

The Law Students' Representative Council

This is the society’s legislative arm which comprises 31 members cutting across all classes in the faculty.

The Executive council

This arm performs the functions, structure and duties of the executive to the Law Students' Society.

The Judicial Council

They include a Chief Justice and eight other justices of the court and they interpret the law in the society. .

The Law Press Organization

This organization serves as a watchdog to the primary arms of the LSS and has since proven to be indispensable.
